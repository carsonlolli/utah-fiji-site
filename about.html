Our Journey

Chapter History

After years away from the University of Utah, Phi Gamma Delta officially reestablished its presence on campus in 2025 and is set to recharter in 2026. We're a brand-new chapter with a founding spirit — every brother here is helping build something from the ground up.

That energy is what sets us apart. We don't carry decades of "the way things have always been done." Instead, we have a group of driven men shaping the culture, traditions, and standards of this chapter from day one — carrying the weight of FIJI's national legacy while writing an entirely new chapter of our own at Utah.

"Every tradition, every standard, every piece of our culture is being shaped right now."The Founding Class · Utah

Founded 1848

The National FIJI Legacy

Phi Gamma Delta was founded in 1848 at Jefferson College in Canonsburg, Pennsylvania. With the motto "Not for College Days Alone," FIJI has grown to more than 120 active chapters across North America — one of the largest and most influential men's fraternities.

Our national organization has produced leaders in business, politics, academics, and public service. The principles established over 175 years ago continue to guide our chapter's commitment to friendship, knowledge, service, morality, and excellence.
