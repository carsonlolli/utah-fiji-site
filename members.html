Get Connected

  • Join the chapter GroupMe & Slack channelsAsk any brother for the invite links
  • Follow FIJI Utah on social mediaInstagram, Twitter/X — stay in the loop

Get Involved

  • Sign up for at least one committeePhilanthropy, Social, Recruitment, Academics, or House Ops
  • Attend your first chapter meetingSundays at 7:00 PM, chapter house — business casual
  • Sign up for your first service eventCheck the events calendar or ask the Philanthropy chair
  • Attend the Built to Lead kickoff sessionFIJI's leadership development program

Know the Standards

  • Read the chapter handbookPolicies, bylaws, and expectations
  • Understand the GPA requirement (2.5 minimum)Study hours mandatory if below chapter average
  • Review the attendance policy (80% minimum)Unexcused absences may result in fines
  • Acknowledge the FIJI code of conductYou represent Phi Gamma Delta at all times
  • Attend the New Initiate Welcome DinnerFormal welcome event at the chapter house

Support & Education

Brother Resources

Tools, training, and support for every brother in the chapter.

Certified Recruiter Training

The gold standard certification for fraternity recruiters — evidence-based recruitment practices, ethical standards, and communication skills.

Substance Awareness

Our chapter fosters a culture of responsible choices. Resources cover risks of alcohol and drug use, harm reduction, and support for those who need it.

Hazing Prevention

Hazing has no place in FIJI. We build men through positive mentorship. Clear definitions, university policies, and reporting procedures are available to all.

Mental Health Support

Resources for depression, anxiety, stress management, and suicide prevention. Brothers are encouraged to seek help without stigma.

Sexual Respect & Consent

Consent is clear, enthusiastic, and ongoing. Resources include education on healthy relationships, bystander intervention, and reporting procedures.

Community Education

Topics that protect our brothers and strengthen our community — bias awareness, inclusion, crisis communication, and ethical leadership.
